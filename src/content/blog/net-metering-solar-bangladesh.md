Bangladesh stands at a critical juncture in its energy transition. With electricity demand growing at over 10% annually and diesel-based captive generation eating into household incomes, rooftop solar with net metering presents a structurally sound answer.

## What is Net Metering?

Net metering allows residential solar prosumers to feed surplus energy back to the grid and receive bill credits in return. It turns a passive consumer into an active grid participant — reducing peak load stress while monetizing idle rooftop space.

## The Bangladesh Context

Currently, BPDB's net metering policy (Circular No. 20/2018) allows up to 70% of connected load to be installed as rooftop PV. However, adoption remains negligible due to three friction points:

1. **Upfront capital** — a 3kW system costs ~BDT 2.5–3.5 lakh without subsidy
2. **Bureaucratic onboarding** — inspection and approval timelines can stretch 60–90 days
3. **Low awareness** — particularly among middle-income urban households in Chattogram and Dhaka

## Our Proposal

The project submitted to the Climate Justice Energy Competition 2023 proposed a three-lever intervention: a revolving credit facility through partner MFIs, a single-window digital approval portal, and a community-based peer ambassador program targeting RMG worker households.

The project won Championship at the competition co-organized by The Daily Star and Action Aid Bangladesh.

## What Comes Next

The policy groundwork is in place. What's missing is last-mile execution infrastructure. If Bangladesh is to meet its NDC target of 40% renewable energy by 2041, residential solar must scale beyond early adopters.
